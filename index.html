Romantic Retreat Relationship Scaffold

This isn’t a form. It’s a threshold. A ritual before the ritual—where the forest starts to lean toward you. Some couples come with a question. Others come without knowing why—only that it’s time. All are welcome. You can complete this together or separately. No pressure. No performance. Just presence.

1. What’s alive between you right now?

Purpose: To begin from honesty, not lack. To reflect the truth of your connection, whatever it is.

Why this matters

You are not arriving to fix anything. You’re arriving to witness what’s already here.

Examples
  • We’ve been laughing more lately—it feels like something’s lightening.
  • Honestly, it’s been hard to connect—but we keep showing up.

2. What does this love already carry that feels rare, sacred, or worth honoring?

Purpose: To name what is precious. To shift into reverence before arrival.

Why this matters

We begin with reverence. Not invention.

Examples
  • We’ve never stopped holding each other through big transitions.
  • This is the first love I’ve ever felt safe in.

3. Is there a memory, quality, or shared experience you’d love to feel more of this weekend?

Purpose: To reawaken what’s been loved before. To give longing a voice.

Why this matters

The forest listens best to longing.

Examples
  • Long, quiet mornings together with no need to talk.
  • Feeling playful again—inside jokes, silly dances.

4. Why this moment? Why now?

Purpose: To locate your arrival in the arc of your love story.

Why this matters

When love asks for marking, something sacred is already stirring.

Examples
  • Our 10-year anniversary—but it feels like more than that.
  • We almost didn’t make it last year. This is our choosing again.

5. What do you both need less of right now?

Purpose: To clear emotional noise and make space for depth.

Why this matters

Clearing space is part of the celebration.

Examples
  • Screens, plans, and other people’s energy.
  • Trying to ‘make the most’ of things. We want to just be.

6. What’s been tender or tired in your connection lately?

Purpose: To name what’s real without trying to fix it.

Why this matters

Intimacy doesn’t require polish. Only truth.

Examples
  • We’ve been loving but low-energy—just getting through days.
  • We feel close, but not very expressive lately.

7. What’s something you’ve made it through together that deserves to be acknowledged?

Purpose: To honor your history as a source of strength and intimacy.

Why this matters

You carry history. Let it be honored, not hidden.

Examples
  • We moved countries. Twice.
  • There was a time we almost gave up. But we didn’t.

8. What kind of moment would feel unforgettable for you this weekend?

Purpose: To plant an emotional seed that the weekend can grow around.

Why this matters

You don’t need to plan it—but naming it invites it in.

Examples
  • Reading a letter by candlelight.
  • A shared bath under stars in total silence.

9. If this weekend became a core memory… what might it be titled?

Purpose: To set a soft intention for what this stay could become.

Why this matters

Giving a name plants a seed of memory.

Examples
  • The Weekend We Really Saw Each Other Again
  • The One Where It All Slowed Down

10. Is there anything you’d like us to know to help your love feel fully held?

Purpose: To create a field of care shaped around your truth.

Why this matters

This is your retreat. The forest bends toward truth.

Examples
  • Please no surprises or performances—we’re quiet people.
  • We’d love something tactile to mark the moment. A keepsake, a ritual.
